Case Study

FindFlix

The Brief

I was given the task of developing an application that enables users to easily search for movies across all of their streaming platforms all while staying in one app.

The Process

Tech Stack

Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, After Effects, InVision, Trello, Miro

Goals

I understand it’s difficult to find which streaming platforms include which movies. I believe a cross streaming platform search app will result in an improvement of the movie streaming experience. I hope that through user interviews and ideation, I can build an app that meets these user needs.

Research

Through user research I hoped to identify our User Persona and the pain points they face that our application can support. I Interviewed and surveyed real users to gain better insight into if my assumptions are accurate. Organize all the data to recognize the key experiences to include that would solve for our real life users challenges.

Interview Summary

Too many options makes me feel overwhelmed
I constantly have to google to find out what’s available for streaming
I want the convenience of everything on one device
I hate endlessly scrolling because I don’t know what I want to watch

Affinity Diagram

With all that data collected we then organized the results using what is called an affinity diagram. This enabled us to clearly organize and combine our interview data into a single view and help us determine the main focus of FindFlix. Problem Statement

Everyone’s lives are over-booked. When our users want to just relax and stream a movie they don’t want to think and endlessly search.

The User Persona

Definition & Ideation

We took our User Persona and Identified what our real problem is and how we can solve that within our app. We discovered that our users are busy they would like to use any spare time they have to unwind, not endlessly scroll through streaming platforms to find their favorite movie.

Proposed Solution

I believe that an app that would compile, filter and suggest movies across platforms for easy navigation would speak to this. We might do this by enabling cross-platform search and filtering based on genre, directors/producers or performers. Doing this will allow our product to help all spectrum of streaming users find movies they want to watch quickly and easily.

User Flow

Within our User flow we first focused on the onboarding and sign in aspect to ensure the user would feel confident while navigating the app.

Prototyping & Testing

I started with wireframe sketches for our app. Since we’re working remotely it was easier to collaborate via Adobe XD rather than hand sketching separately. This gave us a jumping off point to show how our app will eventually look and flow.

MidFi Testing and Iterations

The iteration process for adding Titanic to the watchlist went in an unexpected direction. The main takeaway from the wireframe testing was that the “star” icon needs to match the “bookmark” icon of our watchlist. So that’s what we did for the LoFi iteration. But during testing only 75% were able to complete the task down from 100% on the wireframes. What we discovered was the icons are much easier to read on the wire frames. Once we added a thumbnail, the bookmark icon got lost. It also didn’t convey that the user is “adding” it to their watchlist. And that’s when we pivoted and went with a “plus” icon because it’s more universally intuitive. Additionally we added a number callout to notify the user a movie has been added.

Conclusions

PLAN FOR SUCCESS, EXPECT FAILURE. That’s how we learned. We anticipated every outcome except all the actual outcomes that occurred when testing. It was an eye opening, humbling experience. Until you test, you have no idea what a user is thinking, feeling and expecting.

OUR APP EVOLVED. It was originally going to just be an aggregator of what’s available to stream on what platform. After conducting user interviews, this grew in scope and gave us insight into what the market is currently lacking.

DON’T FALL IN LOVE WITH YOUR DESIGN. It will constantly be changing and evolving. Nothing is off limits, everything is up for deletion.

SAVE YOUR ITERATIONS. Keeping a record of the evolution of your design documents your progress and reminds you of the steps you took towards improvement. Those who don’t save their iterations are doomed to iterate again and again.
